Palolo Worms

Originated at Samoan.

Brief history

Historically, Palolo worm, any of various segmented marine worms of the families Eunicidae and Nereidae (class Polychaeta, phylum Annelida). The palolo worm exhibits unique breeding behaviour: during the breeding season, always at the same time of year and at a particular phase of the Moon, the worms break in half; the tail section (the “epitoke”), bearing reproductive cells, swims to the surface, where it releases eggs and sperm. Tens of thousands of epitokes swarm and release gametes simultaneously, attracting predatory fish and humans. The front section of the worm (the “atoke”) remains below in the substrate.

Palolo is the edible portion of a polychaete worm (Eunice viridis) that lives in shallow coral reefs throughout the south central Pacific, although they do not swarm at all of these locations.

How does it taste like?


With the first taste of palolo we can understood the Samoans' love for it. Certainly it suggested a salty caviar, but with something added, a strong, rich whiff of the mystery and fecundity of the ocean depths.

Method of Cooking

Palolo is need to be frozen overnight. Then fry it with some onions and egg. No need for salt or any other spices. The palolo’s natural, deep sea flavour was plenty strong enough to beautifully season this dish. Then serve it and enjoy!
