Pineapple Lumps

Originated at New Zealand.

A Brief History

Regina confectionery company in Oamaru for the first time presents pineapple chunks in about 1952-54. Charles diver, head pastry production Manager floor of the hotel Regina, who would later formulate other classic kiwi sweets, was assigned the task of using waste from other candy time. One sweet in particular, an early version of the chocolate fish with pineapple with the taste of marshmallow medium resulted in the marshmallow left, the diver is used to create pieces of pineapple. The name of the tool was changed to pineapple lumps Regina in the early 1960-ies to give it a more catchy title. Received Cadbury pineapple lumps name and began manufacturing and selling them under their own brand and pascall.

Pineapple lumps or slices of pineapple in the chocolate confection with a soft, chewy pineapple-flavoured middle from New Zealand. They are often identified as Kiwiana.

How does it taste like?


Pineapple lumps or pineapple chunks are a chocolate-covered confection with a soft, chewy pineapple-flavoured middle from New Zealand. They are often identified as Kiwiana.

How to Create Pineapple Lumps?

Melt the marshmallows and water in a large bowl over simmering water. Take off the heat and stir the mixture. Leave to cool for a few minutes. Sift in half the Chelsea Icing Sugar to help stiffen the paste. This is quite tricky as the mixture is very sticky. Just keep adding icing sugar to help unstick things. When the dough is firm enough to handle, transfer to a tabletop sprinkled with a generous amount of icing sugar.

Knead the dough while slowly sieving more icing sugar over the dough as necessary. Add yellow colouring and pineapple flavouring to taste.

Roll to the thickness that you want and cut into rectangles or any other shape you want. Melt the chocolate and leave to cool for a little while (so it doesn't melt the pineapple lumps). Dip each piece in chocolate and leave to harden.
