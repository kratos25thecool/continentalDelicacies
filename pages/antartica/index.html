Delicacies in Antartica

by Angelo Regis C. Orate

Antarctica brings to mind a number of enticing images: stunning, undulating landscapes unlike any others in the world, massive ice cliffs dramatically dropping off into the frigid ocean, unusual wildlife including eight separate species of penguins.

The frozen continent might not be your first thought when it comes to amazing meals. Largely because Antarctica is a place where food does not grow. It isn't populated except by visitors who stay for a few months. There are no farms and no edible vegetables growing there. Furthermore, the wildlife is protected making it illegal to eat them.

Most people in Antarctica stay in national bases. They eat food shipped in from their own country and bought at ports en route. All food are prepared by qualified cooks/chefs in modern kitchens with modern cooking equipment. Fresh fruit and vegetables are available when ships or aircraft arrive from beyond Antarctica. Some bases also have hydroponic growing systems which provides a small supply of fresh vegetables and herbs grown under lights in the dark Antarctic winter.
