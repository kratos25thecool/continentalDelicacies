Dreams from Myself

Childhood Years

1I was born in 2001 into a nuclear family. 2I lived with my father, mother, and soon after, my sister. 3I am very thankful to my parents for showering us with love and more than our needs. 4My mother used to work abroad and was essentially a very adventurous person. 5Despite that, she decided to stay here in the Philippines to properly raise us herself. 6My father was one to both work and pursue knowledge. 7He works as a professor in UE Manila and, at the time, completing his Doctorate degree. 8My sister was born in 2004, she always liked drawing and has grown to be quite the artist. 9I started Nursery in a learning center at the age of four. 10My mother has always been pushy about academics so by the time I finished nursery, I was able to read and write. 11It was the reason why I was able to get accelerated.
1In my childhood, I was able to play outside a whole lot. 2My friends and I used to play outdoor games almost everyday. 3Games like hide-and-seek, tom sawyer, agawan base, mataya-taya, patintero, tumbang preso, and many more. 4The one I enjoyed the most was hide-and-seek. 5It’s because I’ve always loved crawling into tight spaces looking for places to hide. 6We used to live in a condominium area so there were lots of children and lots of places to hide in. 7In the early 2010s, there was some news about earthquakes hitting our area. 8Seeing as some of those condo buildings were starting to crack, we decided to move. 9From our modest condominium, my parents were able to afford a nice home in a multiplex subdivision where we currently reside. 10I remember how sad I was before moving because I might not be able to see my friends again.

Teenage Years

1After we moved I, of course, had to change schools. 2Admittedly being socially inept, it was a struggle to fit in. 3Since everyone in my new school already knew one another since elementary school. 4As I began to move into my adolescent years, I tried to become more independent. 5Despite the difficulties, I managed to make some friends 6Being honest, I was very influenced by them. 7Well, we didn’t do anything bad but my interests have started to shift. 8I became more into online gaming and anime. 9The complete opposite of my childhood when I used to run outside the whole day, I started to become an indoor person. 10Now I spend most of my time on my computer and phone. 11Even so, I feel like these highschool friends I made will last a lifetime. 12Even when I am already in college, we still contact and talk with each other almost everyday.
1After finishing high school, I entered University of the East for my senior year. 2I managed to make some friends for myself this time. 3It was a relatively large group. 4There were about ten of us. 5It was fun, getting to know all sorts of people. 6But unfortunately, my friend group grew apart by graduation. 7Still though, I feel lucky to be able to contact them every once in a while. 8One of them also continued college in UE as a GameDev student.

College Years

1I started college at 17 and enrolled as a Computer Science student. 2I know I have been influenced to start online gaming. 3But through that influence, I found my interest in computer programming. 4That and future prospects are what pushed me to pursue computer science. 5As for my academics, my mother has always told me to follow my fathers footsteps and study as hard as I can. 6Considering my father accomplished Summa cum Laude, I am not very confident I can follow him. 7I may have gotten his smarts, but not his hard work. 8I will admit that I am a lazy bum. 9This activity is proof of that, which I did four hours into the deadline.
1My first year of college went well enough. 2Although I did not do too well in the entrance exam, I managed to become a candidate for a college scholarship for the whole year. 3Well, not that I needed it as I don’t need to pay tuition as part of my father’s benefits. 4For my second year, we all know how the pandemic hit every one of us. 5Although, I haven’t really felt down because of the lockdowns as I am an indoor person. 6This whole 2nd year being online classes has given me much more free time. 7I used to commute from home to school which is about 2 hours each way because of the traffic. 8But I’ve got to agree with others, I think that classes being online have made learning more difficult. 9This is because of the amount of work that keeps piling up. 10Proper time management and not slacking off would definitely help though. 11I’m now treating these times as a chance to improve my character and learn how to do actual work.
